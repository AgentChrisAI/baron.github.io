The ICP Domination Framework

From Data to Deep Desire

Overview

Six stages from market terrain to buying trigger:

  1. Market Landscape Mapping
  2. Segmentation & Opportunity Selection
  3. Demographic Profiling
  4. Psychographic & Behavioral Analysis
  5. Pain & Desire Excavation
  6. Buying Trigger & Messaging Map

Stage 1 — Market Landscape Mapping

Understand the terrain before choosing a target.

Before defining the customer, define the battlefield.

1. Identify the Market Category

  • What industry are you in?
  • What sub-markets exist within it?
  • What adjacent markets influence buying behaviour?

Example — Online Fitness:

  • Busy professionals
  • Postpartum moms
  • Entrepreneurs
  • 40+ men with hormonal weight gain

2. Study Competitor Positioning

Analyse each competitor for:

  • Who are they targeting?
  • What outcome are they promising?
  • What angle are they using?
  • What objections do they address?

Look for:

  • Over-served segments — crowded, commoditised
  • Under-served segments — ignored, underrepresented
  • Gaps in messaging — pain nobody is naming
  • Emotional blind spots — desires nobody is speaking to

3. Demand & Intent Signals

Mine these sources:

SourceWhat to Look For
Google TrendsRising search terms
YouTube commentsEmotional reactions, frustrations
Reddit threadsRaw, unfiltered language
Amazon reviewsComplaints about existing solutions
Facebook groupsRepeated questions and venting
Sales call transcriptsObjections and hesitations
Competitor testimonialsWhat people valued most

Collect raw emotional language. Do not summarise yet.

Look for:

  • Repeated frustrations
  • Emotional vocabulary
  • Failed attempts
  • Specific phrases they use to describe their problem

Stage 2 — Segmentation & Opportunity Selection

Choose the highest-leverage segment.

Not all audiences are equal. Score each segment:

FactorScore (1–5)
Urgency of pain
Purchasing power
Ease of targeting
Growth potential
Emotional intensity
Competition saturation (lower = better)

The Ideal ICP Has All Four:

  1. ✅ Has money
  2. ✅ Has pain
  3. ✅ Knows they have pain
  4. ✅ Is actively searching for a solution

Missing any one of these = difficult market.

Stage 3 — Demographic Profiling

Build structural clarity.

Surface-level but necessary. Define:

  • Age range
  • Gender (if relevant)
  • Location / market
  • Income range
  • Occupation
  • Education level
  • Family status
  • Stage of business (B2B)
  • Years of experience (B2B)

⚠️ *Demographics explain who. They do not explain why.*

Don't stop here.

Stage 4 — Psychographic & Behavioral Analysis

This is where real marketing power lives.

1. Identity Layer

QuestionAnswer
How do they see themselves now?
How do they want to be seen?
What identity are they growing into?
What identity are they afraid of becoming?

Example — Entrepreneur:

  • Wants to be seen as: successful, respected, competent
  • Afraid of: looking like a failure, being dependent, wasting years

2. Belief System

Identify the beliefs shaping their decisions:

  • What do they believe about success?
  • What do they believe about money?
  • What do they believe about your industry?
  • What myths do they hold as truth?

Common examples:

  • "I need more followers to make money."
  • "Paid ads are too risky."
  • "I'm just bad at sales."
Beliefs determine resistance. You can't overcome an objection you don't understand.

3. Emotional Drivers

Find the dominant emotion powering their decisions:

  • Fear
  • Shame
  • Frustration
  • Ambition
  • Envy
  • Urgency
  • Overwhelm
Strong markets are emotional markets.

4. Behavioural Patterns

QuestionAnswer
Where do they consume content?
Who do they follow / trust?
Do they binge-research or impulse buy?
Do they value speed or certainty?
DIY or done-for-you?

This determines your funnel design, offer format, and sales process.

Stage 5 — Pain & Desire Excavation

The most important layer.

Pain Layers

Move through all three levels:

Layer 1 — Surface Pain

Observable problems

  • "Not getting enough clients"
  • "Struggling with my weight"
  • "Low energy all the time"

Layer 2 — Core Pain

Internal frustration

  • "I feel completely stuck."
  • "I'm falling behind."
  • "I'm wasting time and I know it."
  • "I don't know what I'm doing wrong."

Layer 3 — Existential Pain

Identity-level fear

  • "What if I never make it?"
  • "What if I'm just not good enough?"
  • "What if people find out I'm a fraud?"
  • "What if I fail and everyone sees?"
High-converting copy lives at Layer 3.

Desire Mapping

Mirror the pain layers with desire layers:

Pain LayerDesire LayerExample
SurfaceSurface DesireMore clients, lose 20kg, higher income
CoreCore DesireConfidence, stability, momentum, control
ExistentialIdentity DesireRespected, free, powerful, successful, secure

Your marketing must speak to all three layers.

Stage 6 — Buying Trigger & Objection Map

Convert insight into strategy.

1. Buying Triggers

What causes them to finally act?

  • Pain threshold crossed
  • External deadline
  • Social proof (seeing someone like them succeed)
  • Fear of opportunity loss
  • Status shift (promotion, life event)
  • Financial pressure
Triggers are emotional spikes. Understand what breaks inertia.

2. Objection Mapping

ObjectionRoot BeliefCounter Mechanism
"Too expensive""I don't believe the ROI is real"Show ROI case studies with specifics
"I don't have time""This will add to my plate"Show speed + done-for-you elements
"I tried before""Programs don't work for me"Case studies of similar people who'd failed before
"What if it doesn't work?""I'll lose money and look stupid"Strong guarantee + risk reversal
"I'm not ready""I'm afraid of committing"Show cost of inaction, not just benefit of action

Advanced Layer — The "Bleeding Neck" Test

A powerful ICP has all five:

  • ✅ Urgent pain (not "nice to fix" — needs to fix)
  • ✅ Money to invest in a solution
  • ✅ Emotional intensity driving action
  • ✅ Active solution awareness (they're already looking)
  • ✅ Frustration with current options (they've tried and failed)

If it's just "nice to have" — it's a weak market.

The Final Test

"This person wakes up thinking about \\\\ and goes to sleep worrying about \\\\."

If you can answer that clearly and specifically — you understand your ICP.

If you can't — you're still guessing.
